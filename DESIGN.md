# Azimut Clinic Design Guide

## Direction
Azimut Clinic should feel like a quiet premium medical service: light, precise, warm, confidential, and calm. The interface should support anxious users by making every next step obvious and visually gentle.

## Visual System
- Backgrounds: layered warm whites, soft ivory, and very light sand gradients. Avoid dark themes.
- Accent colors: use muted burgundy and clinical gold sparingly for actions, markers, and highlights.
- Typography: Montserrat ExtraLight / Light for the current site character. Headings stay light, spacious, and calm.
- Layout: generous whitespace, disciplined grids, and compact repeated UI. Cards should feel refined, not heavy.
- Corners: 14-24px for premium content surfaces; smaller radii for dense form controls.
- Shadows: soft vertical shadows only. Avoid harsh glow, thick borders, or saturated blocks.

## Components
- Primary buttons use a pale gold/burgundy blend and subtle lift on hover.
- Cards use translucent white surfaces with fine gold borders and a small accent line on interaction.
- Accordions should open calmly, with clear arrow affordance and enough internal padding.
- Floating widgets must feel native to the site: warm glass surfaces, gentle shadow, and no layout shifts.
- Mobile should be lighter and more compact than desktop, with no decorative overload.

## Motion
- Use short, calm motion: fade, small translate, soft reveal, and slow background drift.
- Avoid aggressive parallax, spinning decoration, or repeated attention-grabbing movement.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## Content Tone
Text should be direct, reassuring, and specific. No filler UI copy, no fake data, no decorative labels that do not help the patient decide what to do next.
